Film.

Film has always been an inspiration for both technology and art. I love to write scripts, in fact, my experience writing helped me out immensely when I was learning to code. I am very into gritty action, sci-fi, and comedy's. However the best films are the ones that have a great characters, ones you can rally behind.

Photography.

The night is a beautiful place when captured with a long exposure... like 20 seconds or more. One of my all time favorite things is to pack a bag with props, a camera, flashlights and hit the road to some remote location. It's something I can do with friends or alone and have a great time. You get to see awesome locations, get insane pictures and almost always have a story to tell after.

Fine Art.

Painting & Sculpture are mediums that I don't dive into very often but I certainly love it when I have the time. That really is what it comes down to for these, hours and hours of time staring at the same thing while it slowly takes shape. The reward is always having a physical thing that you created in your house. Something nostalgic about these pieces as well.

Animation.

I worked on my first animations at nine years old with my dad when he would get home from work. Back then I was working on 3dMax, the industry and tools have changed quite alot. Since then I have migrated to Maya and continued to animate for non-professional films, and shorts. I have done 3d, 2d, and Stop motion animation, they are all very fun mediums and something I see myself always dabbling with.

Comics.

One word, Marvel. They are killing the game, and have always been my favorite. No offence everyone else creating comics, but even you have to respect what Marvel has built. I have been writing and creating tons of concept art for a series that I would like to contribute to the comic book universe. If your interested in checking out its development click on the social media below I'll be posting updates there.
